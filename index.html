Overview

ViSTR-Bench evaluates whether multimodal large language models can reason from continuous visual cues in dynamic scenes. This page introduces the benchmark through its task definition, task and data distribution, construction pipeline, and evaluation results.

4 reasoning dimensions
12 subtasks
1,093 video QA pairs

Task Definition

Each ViSTR-Bench task requires models to aggregate temporal evidence and make qualitative judgments about motion states, spatial relations, future outcomes, or latent physical properties.

Motion Perception

Vehicle Movement and Relative Velocity evaluate whether models can distinguish true object motion from apparent visual changes caused by camera movement or viewpoint variation.

Spatial Relations

Ego Motion and Passage Feasibility require models to account for camera motion, object positions, scene layout, and spatial clearance over time.

Outcome Prediction

Basketball, Soccer, Golf, Billiards, and Swimming tasks test whether models can extrapolate trajectories and infer outcome-level consequences before final outcomes are visible.

Physical Dynamics

Jenga Stability, Mikado Dependency, and Knot Type examine latent physical states such as stability, contact dependencies, and knot structure.

Task Distribution

ViSTR-Bench contains 1,093 video question-answer pairs across four reasoning dimensions and 12 subtasks, collected from public datasets, curated web videos, and self-collected recordings.

Benchmark Construction

ViSTR-Bench is constructed through a systematic multi-stage pipeline, including data collection, video preprocessing, question-answer pair generation, and human quality control.

01

Data Collection

Videos are collected from public datasets, curated web videos, and task-specific recordings across tabletop, indoor, and outdoor scenarios.

02

Video Preprocessing

Event localization, visual prompting, and outcome truncation remove irrelevant context, referential ambiguity, and answer-revealing frames.

03

QA Pair Generation

All instances are formulated as qualitative binary-choice questions using task-specific templates and randomized candidate ordering.

04

Human Quality Control

Expert annotators verify visibility, temporal sufficiency, objectivity, and non-triviality for reliable benchmark samples.

Evaluation Results

Comprehensive evaluation shows that current MLLMs still struggle with continuous visual cues, subtle motion perception, future outcome prediction, and latent physical reasoning.

Best MLLM 59.8%

Seed-2.0-Pro-thinking achieves the highest overall accuracy among evaluated models.

Frequency Baseline 57.2%

The best model is only 2.6 percentage points above the answer-frequency baseline.

Human Evaluation 89.0%

Human performance remains far above current MLLMs on dynamic spatial-temporal reasoning.
